Yeast Extract Peptone Glycerol+Ethanol+Succinate (Also called YEPEG)

This medium can be used to test the respiratory capability of yeast strains. They seem to grow faster
on this medium than on YP glycerol or YP ethanol.

This protocol can be found on BIONET MOLBIO YEAST newsgroup in a [post](https://groups.google.com/g/bionet.molbio.yeast/c/MwOD4mHKRs8/m/zl7HOTgX0YgJ)  by Aidan Weatherill.

1.  Dissolve 10 g of sodium succinate (270.15 g/mol) in 500 ml of distilled H20
2.  Add 	 10 g yeast extrac
			 20 g bacto-peptone
			 21 ml of glycerol (87% or equivalent)
3.  Adjust pH to 5.5 with 1 M base
4.  Add 20 g agar for solid medium
5.  Add distilled H20 to 1000 ml
6.  Autoclave
7.  When cooled down add 30 ml 95% ETOH and mix
